Who I Am

Rayco Leon

English teacher, researcher, Erasmus coordinator, and educational innovator based in the Canary Islands.

I am an English teacher, researcher, and internationally minded educator from the Canary Islands. My professional journey has taken me through different countries, school systems, and educational stages, giving me a broad and practical understanding of how students learn in different contexts.

I have experience teaching across Infantil, Primary, Secondary, adults, and international learning environments. I have also worked in England, where I gained valuable experience with GCSE and A-Level students, as well as with learners from diverse linguistic and cultural backgrounds. These experiences have shaped me into a flexible teacher who can adapt to different ages, levels, needs, and educational expectations.

My background combines English language teaching, CLIL, international education, inquiry-based learning, and educational innovation. I enjoy creating lessons that are clear, active, and meaningful, where students are encouraged to communicate, think critically, and become more independent in their learning.

Having lived and worked in different countries, I understand education as something deeply connected to culture, language, identity, and real-life experience. This international perspective helps me bring openness, adaptability, and curiosity into my teaching.

Alongside my classroom experience, I am developing my academic and professional work around AI literacy in education. I am interested in how teachers and schools can use technology responsibly and effectively, always with a clear pedagogical purpose.

My goal is to contribute to modern, student-centred learning environments where students feel supported, challenged, and motivated to grow academically and personally.
