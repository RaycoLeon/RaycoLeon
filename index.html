Reading method

Before, during, and after

1

Before reading

Look at the title, predict the topic, and write three words you expect to find.

2

While reading

Underline new words, use context clues, and choose one sentence that feels important.

3

After reading

Summarize the main idea in two sentences and answer the discussion question.

Cool feature

Weekly Reading Challenge

Students can use this mini tracker to turn reading into a visible habit. Complete four boxes in one week and bring your best answer to class.

Weekly activity

Reading Journal

Choose one article, read it carefully, and complete these four parts in your notebook or on Classroom.

  1. Write the title, level, and date.
  2. Copy eight useful words and write a sentence with each one.
  3. Write a five-sentence summary in English.
  4. Answer the discussion question with your own opinion.

Who I Am

Teacher, researcher, and educational innovator.

I am an English teacher, researcher, and internationally minded educator from the Canary Islands. My professional journey has taken me through different countries, school systems, and educational stages, giving me a broad and practical understanding of how students learn in different contexts.

I have experience teaching across Infantil, Primary, Secondary, adults, and international learning environments. I have also worked in England, where I gained valuable experience with GCSE and A-Level students, as well as with learners from diverse linguistic and cultural backgrounds.

My background combines English language teaching, CLIL, international education, inquiry-based learning, and educational innovation. I enjoy creating lessons that are clear, active, and meaningful, where students are encouraged to communicate, think critically, and become more independent in their learning.

My goal is to contribute to modern, student-centred learning environments where students feel supported, challenged, and motivated to grow academically and personally.
